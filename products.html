Section 1 · US Stocks

What are US Stocks?

US Stocks allow investors to participate in companies listed on US exchanges. These may include businesses from technology, AI, semiconductors, healthcare, consumer, financial services and other global sectors.

Section 2

Why US Stocks?

Five reasons US-listed equities deserve a place in an Indian investor's portfolio.

Global Companies

Many of the world's largest companies — across software, hardware, healthcare and consumer brands — are listed in the US.

Sector Depth

Strong exposure to technology, AI, semiconductors, biotech and global consumer brands you cannot easily access at home.

Portfolio Diversification

Adds international market exposure that helps reduce concentration risk in a single economy.

USD Exposure

Provides dollar-linked investment exposure as part of an overall currency-aware portfolio.

Long-Term Themes

Useful for investors looking at global innovation themes such as AI, cloud, semiconductors and clean energy.

Section 4 · US ETFs

What are US ETFs?

US ETFs help investors gain exposure to a basket of stocks, sectors, indices or themes through a single listed instrument. They are useful for investors who want diversified exposure instead of selecting only individual stocks.

Section 5

Why US ETFs?

Five reasons ETFs are a popular starting point for global investing.

Diversified Exposure

Invest in a basket of securities through one instrument — multiple companies in a single trade.

Simple for Beginners

Easier than selecting multiple individual stocks — start with a broad index ETF and build from there.

Index Exposure

Invest in broad US market indices like the S&P 500 or Nasdaq-100 with a single ticker.

Sector Exposure

Explore sectors like technology, healthcare, energy and financials through sector-specific ETFs.

Thematic Exposure

Explore themes like AI, semiconductors, clean energy and dividend investing through theme-based ETFs.
